Chinedu Efoagui, 38-year-old software programmer from Nigeria, was booked at the Cobb County Adult Detention Center in Georgia, after a confrontation with police at a traffic stop.He was imprisoned for more than a year waiting for trial before he died of a pulmonary embolism, only because he could not pay $15,000 bail. He was among 300 people across the U.S. since 2008 died after languishing in local jails for at least a year - never convicted of their charges. One reason they were stuck behind bars is America's bail system: It frees defendants with the money and locks away those without. This spring the jail population in America plunged by 11% because of the mass release to contain the coronavirus pandemic.
